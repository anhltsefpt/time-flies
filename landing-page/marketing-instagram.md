Use a Linktree-style page (or custom page at `finitetime.app/links`) with the following structure:

5. **Support / FAQ** -- Contact page for customer questions

### Highlight Covers (5 Categories)

All highlight covers use the same design system: #0A0A0F background, single thin-line icon in #F97316 (orange), centered. Clean, monoline icon style.

| Highlight | Icon | Content |

### Profile Grid Layout -- First 9 Posts

The first 9 posts form a cohesive 3x3 grid when viewed on the profile. Layout strategy:

```
Row 1 (most recent):
[A1] Dark typographic    [A2] App screenshot     [A3] Dark typographic
     quote/stat                (Day view)               quote/stat

Row 2:
[B1] App screenshot      [B2] Orange accent       [B3] App screenshot
     (Life Map)               hero image               (Events view)

Row 3 (oldest):
[C1] Dark typographic    [C2] App screenshot      [C3] Brand launch
     quote/stat                (Year heatmap)           announcement
```

**Pattern:** Alternating between dark typographic posts (columns 1 and 3) and app screenshots (column 2, plus B1 and B3). The center post (B2) is a high-contrast orange accent piece that anchors the grid. This creates a checkerboard-like rhythm that looks intentional when viewing the profile.

### Overall Feed Aesthetic

- **Background:** Always #0A0A0F or near-black. Never white, never light gray.
- **Primary Accent:** #F97316 (orange) used sparingly -- for key numbers, highlights, CTAs
- **Text Color:** White at varying opacities (100%, 72%, 50%, 35%) to create depth hierarchy
- **Photography Style:** If lifestyle images are used, they must be desaturated, dark-toned, high contrast. Think: person silhouetted against a window at dusk, overhead shot of a desk at night with a single lamp.
- **Filter/Treatment:** All images processed to match the app's dark palette. Blacks crushed, highlights pulled down, slight orange/amber color grade on highlights only.
- **Typography:** Outfit for headlines and body text. JetBrains Mono for numbers, statistics, and data.
- **Grid Consistency:** Every post must look like it belongs on the same feed. If you cover one post with your thumb, the remaining 8 should still feel cohesive.

## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Time Perspective

**Theme:** Confronting the math of mortality. Hard-hitting statistics about how much time we have (and waste) presented as beautiful, shareable graphics.

**Visual Style:** Pure black background. Large monospaced numbers in #F97316. Supporting text in white at 50-72% opacity. Minimal. One stat per image. The number is the hero.

**Examples:** "You have roughly 27,375 days. You have used ~10,000." / "The average person spends 26 years sleeping." / "You have ~40 summers left."

**Posting Frequency:** 2-3 per week

---

### Pillar 2: App Showcase

**Theme:** The product itself. Screenshots, screen recordings, feature deep-dives, and UI close-ups that demonstrate why Finite is worth downloading.

**Visual Style:** App screenshots on #0A0A0F background with subtle device mockup (or floating, no device frame for a cleaner look). Thin orange border or glow around the screenshot. Supporting text below or beside the screenshot in Outfit font.

**Examples:** Day countdown ticking live / Year heatmap filling up / Life Map with phases labeled / Event card with urgency glow pulsing

**Posting Frequency:** 2 per week

---

### Pillar 3: Intentional Living

**Theme:** Philosophical, motivational content that aligns with the app's ethos. Stoic philosophy, productivity wisdom, intentional living advice. Not generic motivation -- specifically tied to time awareness.

**Visual Style:** Textured dark backgrounds (subtle grain or noise). Quotes in Outfit font, white, with key words in #F97316. Attribution in JetBrains Mono at smaller size. Sometimes paired with a moody, desaturated photograph taking up one-third of the frame.

**Examples:** Marcus Aurelius quotes about time / "The cost of saying yes to everything is saying no to what matters" / Practical frameworks like the Eisenhower Matrix reframed through time awareness

**Posting Frequency:** 2 per week

---

### Pillar 4: Community & Social Proof

**Theme:** Real users, real impact. Testimonials, App Store reviews, user-submitted screenshots, "how I use Finite" stories, and community milestones (download numbers, ratings).

**Visual Style:** Screenshots of reviews on dark background with subtle orange highlight on key phrases. User screenshots (with permission) framed in the brand aesthetic. Milestone announcements with large monospaced numbers.

**Examples:** "This app genuinely changed how I plan my weeks" (5-star review graphic) / User screenshot of their Life Map with their commentary overlaid / "10,000 downloads" milestone post

**Posting Frequency:** 1-2 per week

---

### Pillar 5: Micro-Education

**Theme:** Quick, actionable tips about time management, habit building, and life planning -- presented as carousels or infographics that people save and share.

**Visual Style:** Carousel format with consistent slide templates. Each slide: dark background, numbered step or heading in orange, body text in white. Clean data visualizations when showing statistics. Last slide always includes the Finite logo and App Store CTA.

**Examples:** "5 things you should stop spending time on" / "How to audit your week in 10 minutes" / "The 168-hour framework: rethinking your week"

**Posting Frequency:** 1 per week

## 10. Aesthetic Guidelines

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Background** | `#0A0A0F` | Primary background for all posts. Never white. |
| **Background Elevated** | `#0E0E14` | Secondary panels, card backgrounds within posts |
| **Orange (Primary Accent)** | `#F97316` | Key numbers, CTAs, highlights, urgency. Use sparingly -- max 15-20% of any frame. |
| **Orange Dark** | `#EA580C` | Gradient partner for orange. Hover/pressed states. |
| **Orange Glow** | `rgba(249,115,22,0.15)` | Subtle glow behind orange elements. Background tint. |
| **White 100%** | `#FFFFFF` | Headlines, primary text, most important content |
| **White 72%** | `rgba(255,255,255,0.72)` | Body text, secondary information |
| **White 50%** | `rgba(255,255,255,0.50)` | Captions, labels, supporting text |
| **White 35%** | `rgba(255,255,255,0.35)` | Tertiary text, timestamps, metadata |
| **White 20%** | `rgba(255,255,255,0.20)` | Dividers, subtle borders, inactive elements |
| **White 8%** | `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)` | Card borders, faint separation lines |
| **Green** | `#22C55E` | Positive indicators, "live" dots, success states |
| **Red** | `#EF4444` | Critical urgency, delete/danger states (use rarely in marketing) |

### Typography Rules for Text Overlays

**Outfit (Primary/UI Font):**
- **Headlines:** Outfit Bold or SemiBold, 36-60pt depending on format
- **Body:** Outfit Regular, 16-20pt
- **Captions/Labels:** Outfit Light or Regular, 12-14pt
- **Letter Spacing:** Tight (-0.5 to -1%) for headlines, normal for body

**JetBrains Mono (Monospace/Data Font):**
- **Large Numbers:** JetBrains Mono Bold, 48-110pt. This is for hero statistics only (e.g., "27,375" or "07:42:18").
- **Small Data:** JetBrains Mono Regular, 11-14pt. For timestamps, dates, percentages.
- **Labels:** JetBrains Mono Regular, 10-12pt, uppercase, letter-spacing +1-2%. For category labels like "UPCOMING" or "YEAR 2026."

**Rules:**
- Never use more than 2 fonts in a single post (Outfit + JetBrains Mono)
- Numbers are always in JetBrains Mono
- Body text is always in Outfit
- No script fonts, no serif fonts, no handwritten fonts. Ever.
- Text alignment: Left-aligned or centered. Never right-aligned unless for specific design intent.

### Photo/Video Editing Presets

**For Photography (if used):**
- Exposure: -0.3 to -0.5 (darker than default)
- Contrast: +15 to +25
- Highlights: -40 to -60 (pull down aggressively)
- Shadows: -10 to -20 (crush the blacks)
- Whites: -20
- Blacks: -30 to -40 (deep, rich blacks)
- Temperature: +5 to +10 (slight warmth)
- Tint: 0
- Saturation: -25 to -40 (desaturated, moody)
- Vibrance: -10
- Split Toning: Highlights -- amber/orange tint (15-20%). Shadows -- deep blue-black (5-10%).
- Grain: Subtle, 10-15 amount, 25-30 size

**For Screen Recordings / App Footage:**
- No color grading needed -- the app's native dark UI is already on-brand
- Ensure screen recordings are captured at the highest resolution possible (screen record on an iPhone 15 Pro or simulator)
- Add a subtle vignette (-10 to -15) to focus attention on the center
- If using a device mockup, place the phone on #0A0A0F background with subtle shadow

**For Text/Graphic Posts:**
- Created directly in design tool (Figma, Photoshop, Canva Pro with custom brand kit)
- No filters needed -- the design IS the aesthetic
- Export at 1080x1350px (4:5 ratio) for feed posts, 1080x1920px (9:16) for stories/Reels
- Always export as PNG for graphics with text (no JPEG compression artifacts on typography)

### Grid Layout Pattern

The Instagram profile grid follows a **column-based pattern** for visual rhythm:

```
Column 1 (Left):    Dark typographic posts -- quotes, statistics, text-heavy
Column 2 (Center):  App screenshots, product shots, visual-heavy content
Column 3 (Right):   Mixed -- carousels (first slide matches aesthetic), community posts
```

When viewed as a 3x3 grid, the pattern creates vertical stripes of alternating content types. This means:
- Every 3rd post (Post 1, 4, 7, 10...) is a dark typographic/stat post
- Every 3rd post starting from 2 (Post 2, 5, 8, 11...) is an app screenshot or product visual
- Every 3rd post starting from 3 (Post 3, 6, 9, 12...) is a carousel or community post

**Preview Before Posting:** Always use a grid planning tool (like Preview, Planoly, or Later) to see how the next post will look in context with the existing grid before publishing.

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use negative space generously. Let content breathe on the dark background.
- Keep orange accent usage to 15-20% of the frame. It should pop, not dominate.
- Maintain the same background color (#0A0A0F) across all posts for seamless grid scrolling.
- Use the monospaced font (JetBrains Mono) for all numbers and data -- this is a core brand differentiator.
- Include subtle depth cues: faint glow behind key elements, soft shadows, layered transparency.
- Test every post at thumbnail size (how it looks in the grid) before publishing.
- Maintain consistent padding/margins within post templates.

**DO NOT:**
- Use white or light backgrounds. Ever. Not even for "variety."
- Use more than 2 colors besides black and white in a single post. Orange + one event color maximum.
- Use stock photography that looks generic. If lifestyle imagery is needed, it must be dark, moody, and curated.
- Add borders, frames, or decorative elements that conflict with the minimalist aesthetic.
- Use gradients that are not part of the brand palette (no rainbow gradients, no neon, no pastels).
- Overcrowd posts with text. If a slide has more than 3 lines of body text, it needs to be broken into two slides.
- Use trendy Instagram fonts or stickers that clash with the brand (no bubbly stickers, no bright colored text overlays in Stories). If using Stories stickers, stick to the minimal/dark options.
- Post low-resolution images or screen recordings. Everything must look sharp and premium.
- Break the grid pattern for a "fun" post. Consistency builds trust and brand recognition.
- Use hashtags in the caption body. Always place them in the first comment.
