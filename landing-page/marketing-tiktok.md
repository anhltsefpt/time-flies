## 2. Content Pillars (5 Pillars)

### Pillar 1: Existential Math (40% of content)
**What it is:** Hard-hitting time calculations that make people uncomfortable. Raw numbers about remaining summers, weekends, meals with parents, sunsets. This is the bread and butter -- the content that stops the scroll and makes people share with the caption "I did NOT need to see this today."

**Why it works:** TikTok's algorithm rewards high save rates and shares. Existential content triggers both. People save it "to think about later" and share it to inflict the same crisis on their friends.

**Content formats:** Text-on-screen reveals, calculator-style breakdowns, "wait for it" number reveals, split-screen reactions.

### Pillar 2: Satisfying App Visuals (25% of content)
**What it is:** Screen recordings of Finite's UI -- the live countdown ticking, the circular progress ring animating, the Life Map grid filling in, event cards glowing with urgency. ASMR energy. Let the design sell itself.

**Why it works:** TikTok rewards watch time and replays. Beautiful, hypnotic UI animations keep people watching. The dark mode aesthetic photographs extremely well on mobile screens. These videos double as product demos without feeling like ads.

**Content formats:** Screen recordings with ambient/lo-fi music, close-up transitions between app screens, "setup my app with me" videos, before/after customization reveals.

### Pillar 3: POV / Relatable Scenarios (20% of content)
**What it is:** First-person scenarios the target audience lives every day. "POV: you realize your 20s are 40% over." "POV: you check how many days until your lease ends and it is 847." Relatable moments that connect the abstract concept of time to daily emotional experiences.

**Why it works:** POV content has massive comment engagement. People tag friends, argue in comments, share personal stories. This builds community and signals to the algorithm that the content is conversation-worthy.

**Content formats:** Green screen with app in background, face-to-camera reactions, text-heavy POV setups, duet/stitch bait.

### Pillar 4: Creator & Community (10% of content)
**What it is:** Duets with creators reacting to their own Life Maps, user-submitted "my Finite screen" compilations, stitches with productivity/self-improvement creators, comment reply videos answering "how many [X] do I have left?"

**Why it works:** UGC and creator content builds trust and authenticity. It also feeds the algorithm with fresh perspectives on the same core message, keeping the brand from feeling repetitive.

**Content formats:** Duets, stitches, comment reply videos, "day in my life" integrations, creator takeovers.

### Pillar 5: Conversion / Soft Sell (5% of content)
**What it is:** Direct product content -- new feature announcements, "how to set up Finite in 30 seconds," pricing breakdowns, premium feature reveals. Always framed as value-first, never as a hard sell.

**Why it works:** After the other four pillars create desire, this pillar provides the logical justification to download. Keep it rare so it never feels like an ad account.

**Content formats:** Quick tutorials, feature reveal videos, "responding to your DMs about the app" videos, comparison posts (free vs. premium).

## 4. Posting Strategy

### Optimal Posting Frequency
- **Weeks 1-4 (Launch Phase):** 1-2 posts per day, 7 days a week. The algorithm rewards consistency during account ramp-up. Post volume is more important than perfection early on.
- **Weeks 5-12 (Growth Phase):** 1 post per day, 7 days a week. Maintain consistency but shift focus to quality and trend alignment.
- **Ongoing (Maintenance Phase):** 5-7 posts per week. Allow for rest days but never go more than 2 days without posting.

### Optimal Posting Times (EST)
Based on the target demographic (productivity-minded millennials and Gen Z in the US):

| Day | Best Times (EST) | Why |
|-----|------------------|-----|
| Monday | 7:00 AM, 12:00 PM | Morning motivation seekers, lunch break scrollers |
| Tuesday | 10:00 AM, 7:00 PM | Mid-morning procrastinators, evening wind-down |
| Wednesday | 11:00 AM, 3:00 PM | Midweek slump, afternoon energy dip |
| Thursday | 12:00 PM, 7:00 PM | Lunch break, pre-weekend planning |
| Friday | 5:00 PM, 9:00 PM | Weekend anticipation, evening scrolling |
| Saturday | 9:00 AM, 8:00 PM | Lazy morning, Saturday night existential hours |
| Sunday | 10:00 AM, 7:00 PM | Sunday planning, Sunday scaries peak |

**Prime time for existential content:** Sunday 7-10 PM (Sunday scaries), weeknights 10 PM-1 AM (late-night thinking hours), Monday 6-8 AM (fresh start energy).

### Content Mix Ratio (Weekly)
For a 7-post week:
- 3 posts: Existential Math (shock value numbers, time calculations)
- 2 posts: App Showcase / Screen Recording (satisfying UI, feature demos)
- 1 post: Trending Sound Adaptation (ride whatever is trending that week)
- 1 post: POV / Relatable (community-building content)

Rotate in Creator/Community content and Conversion content as they become available -- replace one Existential post every other week.

### Caption Strategy
**Length:** 1-2 lines maximum. TikTok captions are secondary to video content but critical for searchability.

**Formula:** [Emotional hook] + [CTA or question]

**Examples:**
- "you have about 50 more christmases. what are you doing with them? #finite"
- "this app ruined my peace of mind and I am grateful. link in bio."
- "nobody asked for this math but here we are. #mementomori"
- "comment your age and I will tell you your percentage. #finite"
- "the life map is not for the faint-hearted. you've been warned."

**Rules:**
- Never use corporate language. No "Download now!" or "Check out our app!"
- Write like a person, not a brand. Lowercase. Casual. Slightly unhinged.
- End with either a question (engagement bait) or a challenge (share bait)
- Include 1-2 hashtags in the caption itself, rest go in the hashtag section

### Going Viral Playbook

**The Viral Formula for Finite:**

3. **Emotional Peak (15-20 seconds):** Deliver the gut-punch. This is the moment that makes someone screenshot, share, or stare at their ceiling. "Sixty-three more times. That is it."

4. **Resolution (20-end):** Offer a path forward. Not a sales pitch -- a perspective shift. "The question is not how much time you have. It is what you do with what is left." Then, softly: "Finite. Link in bio."

- Repost top-performing content with slight variations every 4-6 weeks

- Share rate (the most important metric -- people send existential content to friends)

## Appendix: Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet

### The Finite TikTok Voice
- First person. Lowercase. Slightly unhinged.
- Talk like someone who just realized they are mortal and cannot shut up about it.
- Never corporate. Never salesy. Never "Download our app today!"
- Instead: "this app ruined my life in the best way. link in bio if you want to join me in this crisis."

### Content Creation Checklist (Before Every Post)
- [ ] Does the hook work in the first 3 seconds with sound OFF? (Most people scroll muted)
- [ ] Is there a clear emotional trigger? (Fear, curiosity, shock, or inspiration)
- [ ] Is the video under 30 seconds? (Unless the format demands longer)
- [ ] Does the text overlay tell the story independently of the voiceover?
- [ ] Is there a reason to comment? (Question, challenge, or controversial statement)
- [ ] Is there a reason to share? ("My friend needs to see this")
- [ ] Is the CTA soft and natural? (Not "DOWNLOAD NOW")
- [ ] Are hashtags a mix of branded + niche + trending?

### Emergency Viral Playbook
If a video starts gaining unexpected traction (10x normal views in first hour):
1. Reply to EVERY comment within 30 minutes
2. Pin the best comment that asks "what app is this?"
3. Reply to 2-3 comments with follow-up videos (same day)
4. Post a "Part 2" within 24 hours
5. Boost the video with $20-50 Spark Ad budget immediately
6. Cross-post to Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts within 12 hours
7. DM creators who commented to explore collaboration
