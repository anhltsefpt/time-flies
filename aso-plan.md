## 7. Ratings & Reviews Strategy

### Current State

The app appears to be new/recently launched. Building an initial base of ratings and reviews is critical for conversion and ranking.

|--------|---------------|----------------|-----------------|

| Total Ratings | 100+ | 500+ | 2,000+ |

| Rating Volume / Week | 8-10 | 15-20 | 30-40 |

### In-App Review Prompt Strategy

Apple's SKStoreReviewController allows a maximum of 3 prompts per 365-day period per device. Every prompt must count.

**Prompt Trigger 1: The "Aha Moment" (Day 3)**

Condition: User has opened the app on 3 separate days AND has created at least 1 event.

Rationale: By day 3, users who are still returning have experienced the core value. They have had the "uncomfortable-then-useful" reaction. Users who created an event are engaged.

**Prompt Trigger 2: The "Milestone" (Day 14)**

Condition: User has opened the app on 10+ separate days AND has NOT already been prompted in the last 30 days.

Rationale: A 14-day retention user is a power user. Their rating will be high because they genuinely value the app.

**Prompt Trigger 3: The "Upgrade" (Post-Purchase)**

Condition: User has just completed a premium purchase AND the purchase was successful AND at least 2 days have passed since their last prompt.

Rationale: Users who pay for an app have the highest satisfaction scores and leave the most positive reviews. Catching them in the moment of commitment maximizes positive ratings.

### Prompt Implementation Details

```
DO prompt:
- After a positive in-app moment (completing an event, viewing a satisfying life stat)
- When the user is clearly engaged (not during onboarding or immediately after opening)
- At a natural pause point (after closing a modal, after scrolling to the bottom of the Today screen)

DO NOT prompt:
- During first session ever
- During the paywall flow
- After a crash or error
- When the user is mid-action (typing, scrolling, creating an event)
- More than once per 60-day window (even though Apple allows 3/year)
```

### Responding to Reviews

**Negative reviews (1-2 stars):**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Acknowledge the specific issue
- Provide a resolution or timeline
- Include the feedback email: contact@finitetime.app
- Never be defensive

Template:
```
Thank you for the honest feedback. [Acknowledge specific issue]. We are working on [resolution/timeline]. If you have additional thoughts, please reach out to contact@finitetime.app -- we read every message and your input directly shapes what we build next.
```

**Positive reviews (4-5 stars):**
- Respond within 48 hours
- Thank the user specifically
- Reinforce the value they mentioned
- Mention an upcoming feature if relevant

Template:
```
Thank you -- this means a lot. [Reference their specific comment]. We are building [upcoming feature] next, and feedback like yours keeps us focused on what matters. Your time is finite. Glad we can help you see it.
```

### Review Generation Tactics

1. **Email signature prompt:** Add "Love Finite? A quick App Store review helps us reach more people" with a direct review link to the feedback email template in the Settings screen.

2. **Social media reviews:** When users share Finite screenshots on Twitter/Instagram, reply asking if they would be willing to leave a review. Provide a direct link.

3. **App update release notes:** End every update's release notes with: "If Finite has changed how you think about time, a review on the App Store helps others find us."

4. **Community engagement:** In any Reddit thread, Twitter reply, or forum where users mention Finite positively, gently ask if they have left a review.

## Final Notes

This ASO plan is designed for an app that has a genuinely differentiated product. Most ASO plans try to make generic apps appear special through keyword manipulation. Finite has the opposite challenge -- it is a special app that needs to be discoverable by people who do not yet know they want it.

The core strategy is:
1. **Own the "life countdown" keyword space.** No competitor owns this comprehensively.
2. **Convert through emotion, not features.** Screenshots and video should make people feel something, not just understand something.
3. **Build a review moat.** Once Finite reaches 500+ reviews at 4.7+, the social proof becomes self-reinforcing.
4. **Expand through localization.** The concept of time awareness is universal. The app that localizes first wins each market.
5. **Ride seasonal waves.** New Year, mid-year, and end-of-year are predictable viral moments for time-awareness content.

Every second counts. The ASO should reflect that.
